What are TPUs and TPEs?

Thermoplastic Polyurethane (TPU) and Thermoplastic Elastomers (TPE) are flexible, rubber-like materials known for their excellent elasticity, impact resistance, and durability. They provide the benefits of rubber-like flexibility with the ease of thermoplastic processing, making them ideal for applications requiring soft-touch, flexibility, and resilience.

TPU typically offers higher wear resistance, chemical resistance, and strength, while TPE provides a wider range of softness and better cost efficiency for non-demanding applications. Both materials can be customised to achieve the desired hardness, flexibility, and performance characteristics.

Common Applications

TPU and TPE are widely used in automotive, consumer goods, and industrial applications. They are commonly found in soft-touch grips, seals, gaskets, medical tubing, over-moulded components, and flexible housings. Their excellent abrasion resistance makes them ideal for sports equipment, footwear, and protective casings.
