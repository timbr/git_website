What Are ABS Plastics? Not Just for LEGO—But That’s a Start

by Tim Browning
3 min read
Aug 21, 2025
Share this

When you pick up a LEGO brick, you're holding one of the most successful applications of ABS plastics in manufacturing history. But what are ABS plastics exactly, and why did this material become the gold standard for everything from toys to automotive parts?

Talk To An Expert

The Goldilocks Material: Understanding ABS Plastics

ABS plastics represent the perfect "just right" solution in the world of thermoplastics. Patented in 1948 and commercially introduced in 1954, ABS stands for Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene - a mouthful that describes the three monomers that create this versatile material.

Think of ABS like Goldilocks finding the perfect porridge. Each component brings something essential to the table:

  • Acrylonitrile provides chemical resistance and thermal stability - not too brittle
  • Butadiene adds impact resistance and toughness - not too rigid
  • Styrene contributes processability and surface finish - creating that "just right" balance

This combination makes ABS plastic material incredibly versatile for manufacturing applications, from injection moulding to thermoforming ABS plastic sheet.

Why LEGO Chose ABS: A Material Match Made in Denmark

LEGO, founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Kristiansen, initially used wood for their building blocks. The company began producing plastic "Automatic Binding Bricks" in 1949, but these early versions were made from cellulose acetate. However, by 1963, they switched to ABS plastics for their iconic interlocking bricks. The choice wasn't accidental - ABS offered the perfect combination of:

  • Dimensional stability for precise fitting
  • Impact resistance to withstand countless building sessions
  • Excellent surface finish for vibrant colours
  • Durability to last generations

The precision possible with ABS injection moulding allows LEGO bricks manufactured decades apart to still fit together perfectly - a testament to both the material properties and manufacturing consistency.

ABS Injection Moulding: Precision Manufacturing

ABS injection moulding has become the go-to process for creating complex, high-quality plastic parts. The material's excellent flow properties and thermal stability make it ideal for injection moulding applications, from automotive dashboards to electronic housings.

The process works particularly well with ABS because the material:

  • Flows smoothly into intricate mould details
  • Maintains dimensional accuracy during cooling
  • Provides excellent surface finish straight from the mould
  • Can be easily post-processed if needed

Flame Retardancy: Safety First

One of ABS plastics' valuable characteristics is its inherent flame retardancy. While not completely fireproof, ABS has a relatively high ignition temperature and tends to char rather than drip when exposed to flame. This property makes it suitable for electrical housings and automotive applications where fire safety is crucial.

For applications requiring enhanced flame retardancy, ABS plastic sheet and moulded parts can be formulated with additional flame-retardant additives without significantly compromising other mechanical properties.

The UV Challenge: Why ABS Stays Indoors

Despite its many advantages, ABS plastics have one notable weakness: UV degradation. Prolonged exposure to sunlight causes ABS to yellow, become brittle, and lose its mechanical properties. This is why you'll rarely see ABS used for outdoor applications without UV stabilisers or protective coatings.

For outdoor applications, ASA (Acrylonitrile Styrene Acrylate) serves as ABS's weather-resistant cousin. ASA maintains similar processing characteristics to ABS while offering superior UV resistance, making it the preferred choice for exterior automotive trim, outdoor furniture, and building applications.

Beyond LEGO: Modern Applications of ABS Plastics

While LEGO remains ABS's most famous application, this versatile material appears in countless everyday items:

  • Automotive interior components
  • Electronic device housings
  • Kitchen appliances
  • Protective equipment
  • Medical device components
  • 3D printing filaments

The combination of properties that made ABS perfect for LEGO - durability, processability, and dimensional stability - continues to make it a top choice for engineers and designers across industries.

Conclusion: The Goldilocks Principle in Action

What are ABS plastics? They're the result of chemical engineering that found the "just right" balance of properties. From their development in 1949 to their adoption by LEGO and beyond, ABS plastics demonstrate how the right combination of materials can create solutions that last for generations.

Whether you're considering ABS plastic sheet for prototyping, exploring ABS injection moulding for production, or simply curious about the material in your favourite childhood toy, ABS plastics continue to prove that sometimes, the best solutions come from finding the perfect balance.
